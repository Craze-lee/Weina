学术报告(界面工程和显微结构设计优化氮化硅陶瓷性能)

单 位：机电工程学院 
报告题目：界面工程和显微结构设计优化氮化硅陶瓷性能
报告人：Paul F. Becher教授
报告时间： 12月04 日（星期二） 下午14：00
报告地点：大学城工学二号馆205室

专家简介
Paul F. Becher教授于1970年获得美国North Carolina State University哲学博士学位。1970-1980任美国海军研究实验室陶瓷加工部主管。1981-2006任美国橡树岭国家实验室金属和陶瓷部主任及Corporate Fellow。1991年获得洪堡学者（资深科学家）。1999-2000任美国陶瓷学会 (ACerS) 主席。曾获ACerS 著名的John Jeppson Award和Ross Coffin Purdy Publication Award。多次获得洛克马丁公司（美国主要的国防承包商）的科技成就奖。2003-2008任美国国家研究委员会顾问委员会主席。目前为美国橡树岭国家实验室Emeritus Corporate Fellow，美国国家研究委员会委员，世界陶瓷科学院院士，美国陶瓷学会Fellow 以及终身会员，University of Houston兼职教授，University of Tennessee研究教授。发表文章280余篇（每年引用超过120次），专利6个。他的“Microstructural Design of Toughened Ceramics”成为美国陶瓷学会110年历史上最有影响力的11篇文章之一。基于他发明的SiC晶须增韧Al2O3陶瓷刀具，成为最成功的产业化 陶瓷刀具之一，年产值超过3亿美元。

Abstract

The advances in the design of toughened silicon nitride ceramics have their origin in the concepts employed with whisker-reinforced ceramics. The difference being that the reinforcement consists of elongated b-Si3N4 grains. The toughening mechanisms involve crack bridging by the larger elongated beta grains including elastic bridging and grain pullout similar to that observed in whisker reinforced ceramics. The typical microstructure consists of a matrix of fine elongated grains in which the larger reinforcing grains are dispersed. The toughness achieved is sensitive to the aspect ratio, size and amount of the elongated grains, as well as to the intergranular films that are present. Combined fracture toughness and strength values of 10+ MPa•m1/2 and 1000 MPa can be achieved; thus these “self-reinforced” ceramics have exceptional potential for many applications. 

Selection of the sintering additives allows one to manipulate the scale of the microstructure. Rare earth oxides are particularly effective not only in tailoring the fracture properties but also factors such as creep behavior and thermal conductivity. Our studies have shown that the “rare earth effect” is associated with their affinity to migrate into the intragranular films and adsorb at the grain surfaces. Those that readily adsorb on the prismatic surfaces enhance the growth of elongated grains. This appears to increase the value of the toughness plateau but neither the rise of the toughness for the growth of microscopic cracks (i.e., the R-curve effect) or the strengths. 

But this rare earth adsorption behavior also influences other properties. The fundamental basis for this behavior relates to the affinity of the rare earth for oxygen versus nitrogen. Those with greater nitrogen affinity migrate to and adsorb on the grain surfaces; those that prefer to migrate to triple points where oxygen tends to collect. The rare earth’s affinity for oxygen over that for nitrogen basically increases as the size of the rare earth ion decreases. Lutecium, an oxygen-lover, migrates to the triple point reducing the thickness of the film and forms a very stable crystalline oxynitride. The result is the most creep resistant material known. 

The affinity of lutecium for oxygen also provides a means of reducing the lattice oxygen content of the b-Si3N4 grains formed during the a to b phase transformation that occurs during densification. As a result, there is the potential for substantially increasing the thermal conductivity of silicon nitride to produce an electrically resistant heat sink with superior mechanical reliability or a high temperature component that also has high creep deformation resistance. What we are learning is that one can tailor ceramics not only at the microscopic level but also at the atomic level to achieve the desired component functionality.
