学术报告(小规模高效分散式能源用陶瓷微型燃气轮机的前景展望)


单 位：机电工程学院 
主  讲  人：Dr. Hua-Tay Lin  （中文名：林华泰教授）
报告主题：Prospects of Ceramic Microturbines for Small-Scale High-Efficiency Distributed Energy Applications  小规模高效分散式能源用陶瓷微型燃气轮机的前景展望
时        间：2012年9月26日上午10：30—11:30
地        点：大学城工学二号馆205室

主讲人简介：
       美国橡树岭国家实验室陶瓷科学与技术部主任兼终身研究员，世界陶瓷科学院院士，美国陶瓷学会和材料学会会员。美国陶瓷学会子刊 “国际应用陶瓷技术杂志”主编。广东工业大学、美国田纳西大学材料工程系和中科院上海硅酸盐研究所兼职教授。担任30多个国际会议的顾问，主持过著名国际会议45个，编辑学术会议论文集18本，发表学术论文130多篇。曾多次获得美国洛克希德马丁公司、美国陶瓷学会、日本科学技术厅、中国科学院金属研究所和印度金属研究所等学术科研机构的科学技术奖励。
主要报告内容：
        主要介绍美国陶瓷微型燃气轮机领域的研究成果及进展，并将介绍和讨论本研究存在的潜在挑战性、针对环境恶化的可能解决方案以及对未来的展望等。

Prospects of Ceramic Microturbines for Small-Scale High-Efficiency Distributed Energy Applications
 
Dr. Hua-Tay Lin
Ceramic Science and Technology Group
Materials Science and Technology Division
Oak Ridge National Laboratory
Oak Ridge, TN 37831-6068
 
     Microturbine engine could provide a high quality, reliable, and less expensive electricity alternative to potentially replace the needs for the construction of large, central power plants, and high and congested voltage transmission lines.  Also, the key drivers for the use of microturbines include better control of energy costs, reduction of greenhouse gas emissions, and the potential to address combined heat and power needs.  Therefore, the application of microturbines for distributed energy applications has been under development for a number of years in the United States (US) and other countries.  One limitation of most commercial microturbine systems is the relatively low electrical thermal efficiency, which ranges from 20 to 30%.  The strategies, which could be implemented to significantly improve engine performance, are to increase the turbine inlet temperature and/or air/fuel pressure.  Advanced ceramic materials have been considered as enabling technology for significantly improving the performance of microturbine engines.  While silicon nitride and silicon carbide ceramics have demonstrated considerable promise for this application due to their superior high-temperature mechanical performance and oxidation resistance, relatively little effort has been placed on utilizing these materials in microturbine engines.  This presention will describe the work that has been done in this area and also currently ongoing ceramic microturbine projects in the US.  The challenges that remain to be addressed and potential approaches for addressing environmental degradation (recession) and future prospects would be presented and discussed.
